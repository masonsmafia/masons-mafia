February 17th, 2020

It has now been almost two months since Mason has started chemotherapy. He has started to lose his hair.

December 26th, 2019

During the fall of 2019 Mason spent quite a bit of time home from school with various illnesses. Many visits to the urgent care confirmed he had the common virus and it would soon run its course. Thanksgiving 2019, was a new experience. I hosted it at my house and Mason quietly kept to himself. Nothing too out of the ordinary. Christmas 2019 approached and Mason was ill again. A quick visit to Urgent Care confirmed another virus. Christmas Eve Mason and I made a quick last minute trip to pick out the gifts for his sisters. He was strangely out of breath and wanted to ride in a cart. Since it was Christmas Eve and chaotic there weren’t any carts available. He made his way through the jewelry and picked out their gifts and was ready to go home. He had always loved to be out and always asked to look at the toys. This time he didn’t ask to even venture to that section. We made our way home and he fell asleep on the couch. That was out of character but he had been sick and was most likely overwhelmed. But his color, he was a strange color. Something I had never seen before. But again he had a virus recently and maybe needed some nutrients. The next morning was Christmas. We opened gifts and Mason participated as usual. He got a bike from Santa and was so eager to ride it. He climbed up on the seat but couldn’t muster the strength to turn the peddles. This bike wasn’t too big, it will likely be too small, soon but he loved it!!! Feeling defeated he parked his bike and tried to walk to the couch. Now all the Mom alarms are blaring! HIS COLOR HIS ENERGY LEVEL Something isn’t right. I talked with my Aunt, Chrissy and told her my concerns. She was concerned and mentioned how often he had been sick since October. That night I didn’t sleep at all. I watched him sleep. Counting his breaths. December 26, 2019. After getting the ok from work;I pack him up and we arrive at the U of U ER. They drew blood, and more and more people flooded his room. Whispers and concern flooded his tiny ER room. My little boy. I knew something wasn’t right. PANIC PANIC PANIC HOLD IT TOGETHER YOU CANT LOSE IT HES WATCHING YOU PANIC Then In what seemed like a split second I heard “Your son has cancer, I’m not sure if it’s lymphoma or Leukemia, but it’s cancer” My heart sank and my head began to spin...
